Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Agentic System

where complex agentic workflows strategically pause to require human intervention for validation, decision-making, or edge-case handling before proceeding

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

A Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) Agentic System is an Agentic System (often some type of agentic workflow) where there are times when the system stops, and more or less ask a human what to do next.

Let's look at some examples to make this clearer:

Example: AI Travel Agent

Imagine you want to take a trip from your home in Vancouver, Canada to Busan (부산), Korea. So you ask an AI Travel Agent:

I want a five-day vacation in Busan for under $2,500.

The AI agent can:

  • search for flights and hotel.
  • build a day-by-day itinerary.
  • Suggest restaurants and attractions.
  • Compare prices.

It is usually up to the developer of the AI agent to decide where to put the human-in-the-loop (HITL) of the agentic system, but for an AI Travel Agent, it would probably be put in the following places so that:

  • It suggests, but you approve which flight to book.
  • It suggests, but you reject hotels you don't like.
  • You ask it to make changes. (Ex: "I would prefer to stay near the beach.")
  • Nothing is purchased until you give permission.

Again, the developer of the AI agent decides where to put the HITL, but these are likely places for an AI Travel Agent.

Example: Family Groceries AI Assistant Agent

Here is another example — a family groceries AI assistant agent.

This AI agent deals with keeping the refrigerator, freezer, and pantry sufficiently stocked with food.

Again, the developer of the AI agent decides where to put the HITL, but these are likely places for this type of AI Agent:

  • The human decides which (if any) grocery stores they were to purchase from.
  • The human decides which brands (if any) they prefer to purchase over others.
  • The human sets the weekly budget for food. (Ex: $180 per week.)
  • The human decides how much the AI agent is allowed to purchase without approval from a human. (Ex: $40.)

The AI agent notice when milk, bread, or eggs are almost gone, finds discounts, adds them to an order, places the grocery order if it stays within budget. Etc.
